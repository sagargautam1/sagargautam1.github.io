    • PROJECTS OUTLINES

    Evaluation of Hydrological and Crop Productivity Changes at the Watershed-scale Given Downscaled General Circulation Model Output
    The future climate is expected to differ from the climate conditions that humanity has designed hydrological systems and agronomic activities around for the past hundred years. In order to ensure adaptation to these new conditions we must first understand the potential range of impacts with as much spatial specificity as possible. Our ability to plan for adaptation responses to future conditions we must advance our ability to accurately model potential future climate variables and corresponding responses from key systems.
    Objectives of current project:
    1. Model hydrological and crop productivity response to GCM output using crop and hydrological model.
    2. Compare projected hydrological and climate conditions to projected crop, livestock and other human demands within the study area to determine if there is a deficit or surplus.
    3. Generate recommendations for expanding the capacity for future model intercomparison projects.
